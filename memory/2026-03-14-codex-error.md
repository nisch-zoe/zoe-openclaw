# Project Fence: Memory & Context Export

## Project Summary
Project "Fence" (internal code name: "Project Vault") is a premium, local-first iOS expense tracking application built primarily for the Indian market. The primary goal is to provide a highly privacy-conscious alternative to cloud-synced financial apps, ensuring all user data remains strictly on-device with optional iCloud fallback. The app targets urban, tech-savvy professionals willing to pay a premium subscription (₹100/mo) for an Apple-native experience. The core scope revolves around a "Safe to Spend" hero metric (replacing generic bank balances), gesture-based UI, and zero-friction logging via Apple Shortcuts automation. The key constraints are maintaining a 100% local-first architecture (no external servers) and adhering strictly to premium iOS interface guidelines (OLED blacks, glassmorphism, haptics). The project balances active Swift/iOS development with a psychology-driven, community-led marketing campaign across Reddit and X/Twitter.

## Main Entities
*   **Fence (The App):** A premium iOS expense tracker focusing on total privacy, local storage, and automated data entry. Its design language mimics a "Pilot's HUD" mixed with a "Social Feed," featuring rich merchant logos, OLED-friendly aesthetics, and heavy haptic feedback.
*   **Target Audience:** Urban, tech-savvy Indian iPhone users (18-35) who frequent Reddit and X. They value data privacy, are highly skeptical of traditional bank/UPI apps tracking their spending habits, and are willing to pay for a high-quality, local-only tool.
*   **"Safe to Spend":** The hero metric and core differentiator of the application. Instead of showing a gross bank balance, it calculates and displays the exact amount a user can freely spend after accounting for upcoming bills, EMIs, and fixed expenses.
*   **The PR Stack (v1.0 Foundation):** The completed initial codebase sequence (PR-1 to PR-8). It encompasses core infrastructure including parser integrity, onboarding flows, local ML inference for category prediction, CSV/PDF exports, iCloud sync, and iOS widgets.

## Important Facts, Decisions, and Assumptions

**Product & Design Decisions**
*   **No Cloud Servers:** The application operates 100% locally with zero external server syncing. Any backup is handled natively via iCloud fallback.
*   **Dashboard Layout:** The UI uses a "Card + Feed" structure. The top 35% is a dynamic, swipeable "Safe to Spend" hero card (deep indigo/purple gradient). The bottom 65% is a scrollable transaction feed.
*   **Visual Identity:** The app strictly follows an "Apple-native" feel. It uses deep blacks for OLED screens, glassmorphic UI elements, SF Pro Rounded typography, and prioritizes squish animations and "odometer" number rolling.
*   **Data Entry:** Frictionless input is prioritized. The app relies heavily on Apple Shortcuts for background transaction logging, supplemented by a gesture-based (drag-to-add) manual entry system. Boring text lists are banned in favor of rich merchant logos and emojis.

**Marketing & Positioning Assumptions**
*   **Willingness to Pay:** There is a viable subset of premium Indian users willing to pay ₹100/mo for absolute financial privacy and a superior UI.
*   **Privacy as a Wedge:** Traditional bank apps and UPI trackers are increasingly viewed with suspicion regarding data harvesting. "No-tracking" is our strongest wedge into the market.
*   **Psychology over Features:** "Safe to Spend" acts as a better emotional hook and behavior-changing mechanic than traditional budgeting limits, graphs, or pie charts.

**Campaign & Growth Facts**
*   **Platform Focus:** Marketing heavily indexes on a "Build in Public" strategy on X/Twitter and organic, helpful participation in Indian subreddits (r/india, r/developersIndia, r/iphone, r/personalfinanceindia).
*   **Zero-Link Policy (Early Stage):** Direct linking in early community posts is strictly avoided to prevent bans. The strategy relies on creating recognizable product names and utilizing conflict hooks.
*   **Launch Phasing:** The rollout is structured in distinct weekly phases: Foundation/Hype Seed (Week -2), Community Building (Week -1), Launch Blitz (Week 0), and Sustain & Iterate (Week +1).
